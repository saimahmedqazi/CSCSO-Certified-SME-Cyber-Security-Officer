### Introduction

A **Cyber Threat Actor** (or **[[Bad Actor]]**) is any individual or entity that intentionally targets digital systems to compromise the security of data or infrastructure. While the term "hacker" is often used broadly, a threat actor specifically operates with malicious intent to disrupt the **CIA Triad** (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability).

In the modern landscape, these actors are rarely solitary; they function as part of a professionalized **[[The Underground Cybercrime Economy]]**, utilizing specialized services to increase the speed and scale of their attacks.

---

### Actor Profiles

Cyber threats are generally categorized by their **motivation**, **skill level**, and **target selection**. You can find the detailed breakdown of each category in the linked files below:

- **[[Cyber Criminals]]** : Financially motivated professionals; the primary threat to most businesses.
    
- **[[Nation States]]** : High-level government-backed entities focused on espionage and strategic advantage.
    
- **[[Hactivists]]** : Individuals or groups driven by political, social, or ideological causes.
    
- **[[Terrorist Groups]]** : Actors seeking to spread fear, propaganda, or disrupt critical infrastructure.
    
- **[[Thrill Seekers]]** : Often "script kiddies" looking for personal satisfaction or social clout.
    
- **[[Insiders]]** : Threats originating from within the organization, whether malicious or negligent.
