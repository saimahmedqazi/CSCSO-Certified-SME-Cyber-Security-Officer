### Definition

**Inadequate Training** is a state where employees lack the necessary knowledge, skills, and awareness to recognize, prevent, and respond to cyber threats. Since **human error** is a factor in over 80% of data breaches, a workforce that hasn't been trained acts as an unintentional "open door" for attackers.

### Explanation

Attackers target SMEs specifically because they know these businesses rarely have a "Culture of Security." Employees in smaller firms often wear many hats and may prioritize speed or convenience over security protocols. - **Password Hygiene:** Reusing the same password across multiple accounts or using simple, easily guessable passwords.
    
- **Safe Browsing:** Understanding the risks of downloading attachments from unknown sources or using "free" software.

### Example

- **The "CEO Fraud" Trap:** An employee receives a fake email from their "boss" asking for an urgent wire transfer. Without training on verification steps, the employee sends the money immediately.
    
- **Social Engineering via Phone (Vishing):** An attacker calls an employee pretending to be "IT Support" and asks for their password to "fix a server issue." An untrained employee is likely to comply.
