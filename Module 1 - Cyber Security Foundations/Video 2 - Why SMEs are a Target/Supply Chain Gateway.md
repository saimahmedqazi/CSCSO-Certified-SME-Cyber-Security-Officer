### Definition

A **Supply Chain Gateway** refers to the role an SME plays as an entry point into the networks of larger, more high-value organizations. Because SMEs often have trusted digital connections to their enterprise clients, they act as a "gateway" that attackers use to bypass the stronger defenses of the ultimate target.

### Explanation

Large corporations usually have world-class cybersecurity. However, they must "open their doors" to vendors, contractors, and suppliers to conduct business. Attackers exploit this trust. Instead of attacking the "front door" of the giant corporation, they hack the "side door" (the SME). Once the SME is compromised, the attacker uses the existing trusted tunnel (like an API, a shared portal, or an email account) to slide into the larger network undetected.
